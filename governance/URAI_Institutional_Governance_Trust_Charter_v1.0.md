## URAI Institutional Governance Trust Charter - v1.0

### PREAMBLE

This Charter establishes the URAI Institutional Governance Trust (hereinafter “the Trust”). The Trust is created to ensure Asset Factory (hereinafter “the Institution”) remains perpetually aligned with its core mission, resilient to external pressures, and committed to long-duration value creation. The Trust is the ultimate steward of the Institution’s foundational principles, designed to outlive founders, management teams, and market cycles.

### ARTICLE I: INSTITUTIONAL MISSION

**Section 1.1: Core Mission.** The Institution’s permanent and non-negotiable mission is to build, maintain, and advance deterministic, governed, and traceable programmable media infrastructure.

**Section 1.2: Mission Lock.** The Core Mission is hereby locked. Any proposal that materially dilutes or abandons this mission requires an Extraordinary Governance Process as defined in Article V.

### ARTICLE II: TRUST AUTHORITY & RESPONSIBILITIES

**Section 2.1: Ultimate Authority.** The Trust is the highest-level governance body, holding ultimate authority on matters of mission integrity, constitutional amendments, and founder-level reserved powers.

**Section 2.2: Core Responsibilities.** The Trust shall:

a) Safeguard the URAI Meta-Constitution.
b) Appoint and oversee the Governance Council.
c) Act as the final arbiter in governance disputes.
d) Oversee the Founder Legacy Vault and its activation protocols.
e) Approve any sale, merger, or dissolution of the Institution.

### ARTICLE III: GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE

**Section 3.1: The Governance Council.** The Trust shall appoint a seven-member Governance Council responsible for overseeing the Institution's operational board and ensuring mission alignment.

**Section 3.2: Founder Protections.** The Founder, during their tenure, is protected by specific rights outlined in the Founder Protection & Employment Agreement, including protections against removal without Cause and the authority to veto strategic pivots that violate the Infrastructure Sanctity Clause.

**Section 3.3: Dual-Class Sovereignty.** The Class-B super-voting shares, representing majority voting control, are held by the Trust, ensuring long-term governance stability.

### ARTICLE IV: INFRASTRUCTURE SANCTITY

**Section 4.1: Foundational Asset.** The Institution’s deterministic architecture, version-locking systems, and audit traceability frameworks are designated Foundational Infrastructure Assets.

**Section 4.2: Protection Clause.** These assets cannot be dismantled, diluted, or compromised for short-term financial gain. Any such proposal is a violation of this Charter.

### ARTICLE V: EXTRAORDINARY GOVERNANCE PROCESS

**Section 5.1: Trigger Conditions.** An amendment to this Charter or a material deviation from the Core Mission requires an 80% supermajority approval from the Board of Governors, full concurrence from the Governance Council, and ratification by the Trust.

**Section 5.2: Public Review.** All such proposed changes are subject to a mandatory one-year public review and comment period before ratification.

### ARTICLE VI: CONTINUITY & SUCCESSION

**Section 6.1: The Founder Legacy Vault.** The Trust is the custodian of the Founder Legacy Vault, a sealed archive containing the Institutional Cosmology, the Founder's Oath, and emergency crisis protocols.

**Section 6.2: Succession Protocol.** In the event of Founder transition, the Trust will oversee the activation of the Intergenerational Governance Council and ensure a stable transfer of leadership, as defined in the 100-Year Institutional Continuity Charter.

**IN WITNESS WHEREOF,** this Charter is adopted and made effective as of this day, to bind all future governance bodies and leadership of the Institution in perpetuity.
